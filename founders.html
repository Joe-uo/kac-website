Meet Our Founders

A dedicated team united by lived experience and professional expertise, driven to make a real difference in the lives of autistic and ADHD adults.

Joy - CQC Registered Manager, OFSTED Registered Manager

I am a Registered Social Worker with an academic foundation in psychology (BSc) and a Master’s degree in Social Work Practice. I have extensive experience supporting vulnerable children, young people, and adults across education, healthcare, and community mental health services. My career has included working in SEN schools, digital healthcare, and NHS community mental health teams, as well as supporting emotional regulation within school-based CAMHS services. Alongside my professional experience, I bring lived experience of supporting a close family member with autism and learning disabilities, which deeply shapes my passion for creating safe, nurturing, and empowering environments where individuals can thrive. Through KAC Care, I am committed to delivering compassionate, person-centred support and addressing gaps in the community by providing high-quality supported living, children’s residential care, and domiciliary care services.

Chioma - Service Development Lead

Chioma's extensive experience in project management and business development at leading companies brings strategic vision and operational excellence to Kind & Compassionate Care. Driven by her lived experience supporting her autistic brother, she channels her professional skills toward creating innovative services that truly empower and support our clients.

Joseph - Co-Founder & IT Lead

Professional Qualifications:

  • Qualified Data Scientist
  • IT Systems Manager

Responsibilities:

  • Builds and maintains IT infrastructure
  • Ensures smooth running of all IT functions
  • Supports digital transformation and innovation

I am a software engineer with a strong academic foundation in Computer Science and extensive professional experience in coding, digital systems, and emerging technologies, including the applied use of artificial intelligence. My passion for this work is deeply personal and shaped by lived experience of supporting a close family member with autism and learning disabilities. This drives my commitment to using technology in meaningful and ethical ways that improve accessibility, strengthen care environments, and create better outcomes for vulnerable individuals and their families.
